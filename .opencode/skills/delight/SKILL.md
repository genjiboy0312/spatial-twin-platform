# Delight

Use this flow when the product is functional but emotionally flat, forgettable, or missing the small touches that make people enjoy using it.

## Preparation

1. Load `impeccable` first.
2. If design context is missing, run `impeccable teach`.
3. Review `../impeccable/reference/interaction-design.md`, `../impeccable/reference/motion-design.md`, and `../impeccable/reference/ux-writing.md`.
4. Confirm what level of playfulness or restraint fits the product, audience, and moment.

## Good delight targets

- success and completion states,
- empty states, first-run flows, and onboarding moments,
- hover, press, drag, and reveal interactions,
- loading or waiting moments that can be made clearer or warmer,
- and hidden details that reward exploration without blocking work.

## Rules

- Delight must amplify usability, not compete with it.
- Keep delight moments quick, optional, and appropriate to the domain.
- Prefer product-specific copy and motion over generic whimsical filler.
- If a delight idea would still feel special on the hundredth use, it is probably on the right track.

## Finish line

The surface should feel more human and memorable while staying fast, legible, and professional enough for its context.
