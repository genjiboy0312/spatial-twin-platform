# Color and contrast reference

Use this reference when the interface needs a more intentional palette, stronger contrast, or a clearer emotional tone.

## Core rules

- Prefer OKLCH or another perceptually sane palette system when the stack supports it.
- Tint neutrals toward the brand hue instead of relying on dead gray.
- Let accent colors stay rare enough to matter.
- Maintain accessible contrast for text, controls, and focus states.

## Good palette habits

- Choose one dominant color family, one supporting family, and a restrained accent.
- Let semantic states remain legible and distinct.
- Make dark and light themes deliberate, not automatic defaults.

## Anti-slop watchouts

- Purple-blue gradient hero treatment by default.
- Pure black or pure white used as large surfaces.
- Gray text sitting on colored fills.
- Over-coloring every component until hierarchy disappears.
